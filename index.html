What is a Spotify Canvas?

A Spotify Canvas is a 3–8 second vertical looping video that replaces a track's cover art in the Now Playing view on the Spotify mobile app. Spotify launched Canvas in 2019 and made it free for every artist. According to Spotify's own data, tracks with a Canvas see a measurable lift in shares, saves, playlist adds and time spent. Canvas only plays on iOS and Android — not on desktop, web player, or smart speakers — so the format is optimised for the phone-screen Now Playing experience.

Canvas is uploaded through Spotify for Artists, not through your distributor. You don't need DistroKid, Tunecore, Amuse or CD Baby to add it — only access to the Spotify for Artists team for the track. spotifyedits.com generates the MP4; you upload it.

How to make a Spotify Canvas (step by step)

  1. Pick the Canvas format tab at the top of this page (selected by default).
  2. Drop a photo (JPG / PNG / WEBP) or short video (MP4 / MOV / WEBM). For a still photo, pick an effect — Slow zoom in, Slow zoom out, Pan up, Pan down, Breathe, or Static.
  3. Adjust the 9:16 crop with zoom and drag. For video, set Start and End — the clip must be between 3 and 8 seconds.
  4. Tick Seamless loop and pick a crossfade length (0.3s, 0.5s, or 0.8s). The tool blends the last frames into the first frames using cosine easing, producing a mathematically seamless transition.
  5. Click Export MP4. The file encodes locally in your browser using the WebCodecs H.264 encoder. Save the MP4 and upload it to Spotify for Artists → your track → Canvas.

How to upload your Canvas to Spotify

  1. Sign in at artists.spotify.com with the team account that owns the track.
  2. Open Music → Releases and select the song.
  3. Click the Canvas tab on the track page.
  4. Upload the 9:16 MP4 you exported from spotifyedits.com.
  5. Save and publish. The Canvas typically goes live within minutes for most listeners.

Loop styles: Continuous, Hard Cut, Rebound

Spotify's official Canvas guidelines describe three loop types and they look very different on a phone screen:

  • Continuous Loop — end blends into start. Looks premium, you don't notice the restart. This is what spotifyedits.com produces with the seamless-loop crossfade.
  • Hard Cut — video just restarts at the first frame. Cheap-looking jitter every cycle.
  • Rebound (ping-pong) — plays forward, then reverses. Works for some abstract motion but reads as obvious on anything with directional movement.

The Spotify Header (sometimes called the Spotify banner) sits at the top of your artist page on mobile and desktop. Minimum 2660×1140 (we also export 3990×1710 for retina). Spotify overlays your artist name and avatar on the lower-left, so keep important visual detail away from that corner.

Spotify Avatar / Profile picture — 750×750

The Spotify avatar is the round profile picture in the header, search results, and listener following lists. Minimum 750×750, recommended 1500×1500. Spotify crops to a circle, so center your subject — anything in the corners gets cut.
